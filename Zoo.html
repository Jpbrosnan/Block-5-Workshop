Bears

Bear Summary:

Bears are found on the continents of North America, South America, and Eurasia. Common characteristics of modern bears include large
bodies with stocky legs, long snouts, small rounded ears, shaggy hair, plantigrade paws with five nonretractile claws, and short tails.

Lions

Lion Summary:

Lions have strong, compact bodies and powerful forelegs, teeth and jaws for pulling down and killing prey.
Their coats are yellow-gold, and adult males have shaggy manes that range in color from blond to reddish-brown to black.

Monkeys

Monkey Summary:

Closely related to apes, monkeys are primates that have a tail and usually live in trees. In some species, the tail is longer than the rest of the body.
Monkeys have a relatively large brain and they use their nimble, grasping hands for tasks such as gathering food and picking apart fruit.

Alligators

Alligator Summary:

Alligators have a long, rounded snout that has upward facing nostrils at the end; this allows breathing to occur while
the rest of the body is underwater. The young have bright yellow stripes on the tail; adults have dark stripes on the tail.
